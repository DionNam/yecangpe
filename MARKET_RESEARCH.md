## 5. Competitor Analysis (경쟁사 분석)

### 5.1 NihongoJobs.com (Japanese Equivalent - Direct Competitor Model)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Business model** | Self-service job board (not a recruiting agency) |
| **Target market** | Japanese-English bilingual professionals in the USA |
| **Operated by** | Ikigai Connections |
| **Job listings** | ~2,500 active ads (daily updates) |
| **Audience** | 20,000+ followers with Japanese/English skills |
| **Pricing** | Starting at **$129** per job posting (one-time fee) |
| **Features** | Job board + social media promotion + newsletter + bilingual interview service |
| **Revenue model** | Employer-pays (job posting fees) |

**Key insight**: NihongoJobs directly connects job seekers and employers without intermediary placement. This is the exact model for "KoreanJobs" equivalent.

- Source: [NihongoJobs.com](https://nihongojobs.com/)
- Source: [NihongoJobs Employers Page](https://nihongojobs.com/employers/)
- Source: [NihongoJobs Job Seekers Page](https://nihongojobs.com/job-seekers/)

### 5.2 Existing Korean Job Platforms for Foreigners

| **KOWORK** (kowork.kr) | Jobs for foreigners in Korea | Focus on in-Korea employment, not global matching |

### 5.3 General International Platforms

| Platform | Limitations for Korean-Language Talent |
|----------|---------------------------------------|
| **LinkedIn** | No Korean proficiency filters; hard to find Korean-speaking talent specifically |
| **Indeed** | Generic platform; no language-specific matching |
| **Glassdoor** | Company reviews focus; not optimized for bilingual matching |
| **Robert Walters** | Corporate recruitment only; expensive, limited Korean focus |

### 5.4 Competitive Gap Analysis

**No platform currently exists that specifically:**
1. Matches Korean-speaking foreigners with Korean companies globally (not just in Korea)
2. Verifies Korean language proficiency (TOPIK level integration)
3. Targets the growing market of Hallyu-driven Korean learners entering the professional workforce
4. Serves Korean companies expanding overseas who need bilingual talent

This is the same gap that NihongoJobs filled for Japanese - **but the Korean market is larger and growing faster**.

## 6. Market Size Estimation (시장 규모 추정)

### 6.1 TAM (Total Addressable Market)

**Global Korean-speaking foreign talent pool:**
- TOPIK test takers (2024): **428,585/year** (growing 15%+ annually)
- Duolingo Korean learners: **11+ million**
- King Sejong Institute enrollment: **239,020** (2025)
- Korean diaspora: **7 million** overseas Koreans
- Estimated total Korean-speaking foreigners (intermediate+): **2-3 million** worldwide

**Korean companies hiring foreign talent:**
- ~3,000 new Korean overseas subsidiaries annually
- Samsung alone: 137,000 overseas employees
- E-7 visa quota: 35,000 skilled workers
- Total foreign workers in Korea: 1.01 million employed

**TAM calculation:**
- Employer side: ~80,000-100,000 Korean companies with overseas operations x avg $500-2,000/year subscription = **$40M - $200M/year**
- Job seeker side (if freemium): 2-3 million potential users
- **Estimated TAM: $150M - $300M/year** (job matching + recruitment services + value-added services)

### 6.2 SAM (Serviceable Available Market)

Focusing on:
- Korean-speaking foreigners with TOPIK 3+ (professional working proficiency): ~500,000 - 1,000,000
- Korean companies actively hiring foreign talent in Korea and overseas: ~20,000-30,000
- **Estimated SAM: $30M - $60M/year**

### 6.3 SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)

Year 1-3 target:
- Initial focus: USA, Japan, Vietnam, China, Uzbekistan (top TOPIK countries)
- Target 5,000-10,000 active job seekers
- Target 500-1,000 employer accounts
- Average revenue per employer: $500-1,000/year
- **Estimated SOM (Year 3): $1M - $5M/year**

### 6.4 Comparison with NihongoJobs

NihongoJobs benchmarks:
- ~2,500 active job listings
- 20,000+ followers/users
- $129+ per job posting
- Focus: USA market only

**Korean market advantages over Japanese market:**
1. Korean language learning is **growing faster** than Japanese (fastest-growing in 5+ major countries on Duolingo)
2. Korean content exports ($13.2B) are significant and growing
3. Korean companies investing $64B/year overseas (growing markets in EU, Middle East, Africa)
4. No equivalent platform exists yet (first-mover advantage)

## 7. Government Policies Supporting This (정부 정책)

### 7.1 Foreign Talent Attraction Policies

**Digital Nomad Visa (F-1-D) - Launched January 1, 2024:**
- Duration: Up to 2 years (1 year + 1 year extension)
- Income requirement: 100 million won/year (~$70,000)
- Family members can accompany
- Alien Registration Card (ARC) provided

- Source: [Digital Nomads Korea](https://www.digitalnomadskorea.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-koreas-digital-nomad-visa)
- Source: [EY - Korea Digital Nomad Visa](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/technical/tax-alerts/south-korea-announces-new-pilot-program-for-digital-nomad-visas)
- Source: [KPMG - Korea Workation Visa](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/gms-flash-alert/flash-alert-2024-033.html)

### 7.2 E-7 Visa Reforms (2024-2025)

**Key changes:**
- **Language requirement relaxed**: Can obtain visa first, meet Korean language requirement within 2 years (until end of 2026)
- **E-7-4 skilled worker visa**: Points-based, 3-year stay, unlimited renewals, quota of 35,000
- **Construction sector**: Hiring quota increased 4x (0.1 to 0.4 workers per 100M won project value)
- **E-7-4R (Regional)**: New region-specific visa for depopulating areas (107 eligible regions in 2025, up from 89)
- **Pathway**: E-9/H-2 holders with 5+ years can transition to E-7-4 skilled worker status

- Source: [Korea Herald - E-7 Visa Reform](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10011795)
- Source: [Korea.net - Regional Visas](https://www.korea.net/NewsFocus/policies/view?articleId=266947)
- Source: [KOWORK - E-7 2025 Salary Requirements](https://kowork.kr/en/blog/e7-visa-2025-salary-requirements-en)

### 7.3 Metropolitan Visa System (2025)

- Local governments can customize visa systems for their economic needs
- Initial targets: international students (D-2) and skilled workers (E-7)
- Aims to combat regional depopulation by attracting foreign talent to non-Seoul areas

- Source: [Korea.net - 2025 Regional Visas](https://www.korea.net/NewsFocus/policies/view?articleId=265020)

### 7.4 Government Content Industry Investment

- Policy financing for content industry: **1.74 trillion won** (2024), 3.5x increase from 2021
- Annual government budget exceeded **1 trillion won** for first time in 2024
- KOCCA targets: 50,000 new content industry jobs by 2030

## 8. Key Takeaways for Business Plan (사업계획서 핵심 시사점)

### Market Opportunity Summary

1. **Explosive demand**: Korean language learners doubled in 4 years (TOPIK 218K->428K), Duolingo Korean has 11M+ learners
2. **Supply-side growth**: Korean companies investing $64B/year overseas, Samsung alone has 137K overseas employees
3. **Policy tailwind**: Korean government actively easing visa rules, investing 1.74T won in content industry, creating regional visa incentives
4. **Demographic necessity**: Korea's workforce is shrinking (birth rate <0.7), foreign workers increasingly essential
5. **No direct competitor**: Unlike Japanese (NihongoJobs), no dedicated Korean-language talent matching platform exists
6. **Hallyu catalyst**: 225M global fans driving unprecedented language learning growth, creating pipeline of future professionals
7. **Proven model**: NihongoJobs demonstrates the business model works ($129/posting, 2,500 listings, 20K users) - Korean market potential is larger

(The only platform connecting Hallyu-driven global Korean-speaking talent with Korean companies expanding overseas)
